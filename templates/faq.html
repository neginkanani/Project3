Frequently Asked Questions

What are the different kinds of breweries?

Here is some common terminology you might encounter when exploring breweries:

Macrobrewery

Macrobreweries are the "big-name" brewing companies whose product can be purchased worldwide. They produce millions of barrels of beer per year. The beer from these companies are often seen as more quantity than quality focused, have a lighter color and a more watery consistency than most craft beers.

Microbrewery

Microbreweries are smaller, oftentimes independently owned breweries selling craft beer. They produce less than 15,000 barrels of beer per year which is mostly sold and consumed off-site. Microbreweries can sell their beer directly to customers or in a two-tiered or three-tiered selling process.

Regional Brewery

Regional breweries fall in between microbreweries and macrobreweries. They serve beer in a fixed geographical location and produce between 15,000 and 6,000,000 barrels per year.

Nanobrewery

Nanobreweries produce less than 2,000 barrels of beer per year. They are considered the starting phase of a brewery before they become microbreweries. Their beer can be produces in batches as small as 3 barrels.

Brewpubs and Gastropubs

Brewpubs and gastropubs are hybrids of breweries and restaurants. The beer is primarly brewed to be sold and consumed on-site alongside food. The brewery must sell at least 25% of their product on-site to be considered a brewpub or gastropub. Gastropubs place more emphasis on the quality of food than brewpubs, and typically sell more gourmet food items.

Meadery and Cidery

Meaderies produce mead, which is an alcoholic beverage created by the fermentation of honey, water, and yeast. Cideries produce cider, which is an alcoholic beverage created by the fermentation of apple juice.

Contract Brewery

Contract breweries are breweries that brew their product on equipment that isn't actually owned by the company. Some companies will start off as contract breweries until they can get their own equipment, and other companies may use contract breweries to supplement their productions.

Other Information:

    One barrel of beer is equal to 31 US gallons, or 117 liters.
    Kegs carry about half the amount of beer as a US barrel.
    Craft beer refers to any beer produced by a small, independent brewery. It is made using traditional ingredients and processes, with an emphasis on flavor and innovation.
